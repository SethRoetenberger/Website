Electronics

I have had several projects that include ESP32s, Arduinos, or raspberry pis, many of which are still ongoing. All the lights that I have include an ESP32 for wireless control.

I created a night-vision camera and monitor setup by using a raspberry pi. I attached a camera module without an infrared filter to the raspberry pi. Most cameras automatically come with an IR filter, which blocks the IR light and would make the project impossible. I then attached infrared lights to the headset. The IR lights would shine and reflect off the surfaces and be collected by the camera which allowed for night vision. I then displayed this video on the raspberry pi's screen which resulted in a night-vision module.

I had an old laptop lying around and decided to turn it into a home server. I did this by setting up ubuntu server on the device and then implemented port forwarding through the Wi-Fi router to allow the files to be accessed from anywhere.

I made a hydrophone based on a tutorial design. When there is sound in the water it causes the piezoelectric ring to vibrate. This vibration creates a small voltage and goes into an amplifier and is then sent to an audio recording device.
