Flamethrowers

The goal with these wrist mounted flamethrowers was to have them be as small and streamlined as possible, while still being large enough to produce a fireball. There were many iterations to the design that each helped get a little closer to the desired result. Some of these design changes came from changing the ignition source from an arc lighter to a high voltage generator. This high voltage generator ended up being the better decision because of its more consistent results. The rest of the main components stayed the same across multiple iterations. The parts that did change were the 3D printed parts and the charged wires.

The final design had a solenoid and arc light connected to push button and 12-volt battery. When the button was pushed, the solenoid opens and releases the butane and the metal prongs, which are attached to the high voltage generator, produce an arc. The final design ended up being extremely low profile and yet still durable to manage the high heat and also accessible for refueling.
