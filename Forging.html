Forging

I designed and built this forge for future projects, but in the process, it became a project. There were many varied materials and concepts that went into making a successful forge. The insulation coating and thickness were important for trapping heat and the burner was placed at an angle to create an even heat all around the metal.

Forging itself has been extremely interesting because it has shown me how different heats can affect steel and other metal, and how even a red-hot piece of metal can still generate cracks and become weak easily.

There have been many different projects that I have forged. There have been several different flowers such as a calla lily and rose. These were fun, because of the way the petals stacked together. I have also forged a curved nautilus which taught me how far steel can be twisted while heated. I have even forged a small shark tooth necklace out of 1040 steel which I then sanded and hardened. There is currently a small hatchet in the works which required me to punch a hole with a chisel through 1.5 inches of steel. All these different projects have taught me distinct aspects of steel and how materials change based on heat.
