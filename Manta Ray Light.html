Manta Ray Light

This light was one of my very first that incorporated using many different materials. It contains resin, 3D printing, wood working, and metal working as well as electronic. I started off by modeling the manta ray in blender. Once I was happy with it, I 3D printed it using my SLA printer and painted it with an airbrush. I then cast the manta ray and a carved base of wood in resin. After that, I created the frame out of pine and an aluminum C-channel. I used the C-channel to hold the LED strip that would light up the model. After attaching the frame, I included the electronics, which have a touch sensor and are controlled by an ESP32, which allows for the light to be controlled wirelessly.
