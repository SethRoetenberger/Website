Metal Working

Metal fabrication has been an area of interest for me for a long time. There are many projects I have made that fall into this category and each of them has helped my skills grow in this area.

It was important for me to understand the differences between aluminum, which is seen in the wolverine claws, stainless steel, in the gauntlets directly below and the Russian scale armor at the bottom, and mild steel, in the trident, Batman gauntlets, and mace. Each of these metals have different properties and require different skills to effectively use.

I used different techniques for each of these designs because they have different properties. I used pins and riveting for some of the thinner sheet metal because MIG welding thin metal is difficult and impractical. There were spot welds in the trident because I could not use thicker steel but still wanted that wide look. The lily pads required a lot of cold forming with a hammer to get good detail and stylization. All these different techniques are important and useful to get the desired look.
