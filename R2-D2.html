R2-D2

3D printing R2-D2 was quite a challenge that took almost a year. It did, however, teach me so much about 3D printing, such as layer specifications, wall thicknesses, and general maintenance as the printer invariably failed from such a massive amount of usage. When I took on this project, I was using an early 2016 RepRap 3D printer which was prone to constant failures, poor extrusion, and terrible bed adhesion. This culminated in a roughly 50% failure rate when making these prints. Over the years 3D printing has increased incredibly. I first upgraded by getting an Ender 3 in 2020 and then a Bambu in 2025. In each one of these upgrades the failure rate has decreased considerably. I do think that starting off with 3D printing early was still very important to me because it taught me so much about how the printer worked as I was always having to repair it.

I am still working on the electronics part of R2-D2, but I hope to have it completed soon.
