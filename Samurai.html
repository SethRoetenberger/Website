Samurai Armor

This Samurai armor was the first armor I ever built, and it was made from 14-gauge steel. This was too thick of a gauge for armor and made it difficult to bend, drill, and wear. Because of this I had a difficult time fabricating the armor. With typical 18-gauge steel, the bending can be done without heat, but with this being my first attempt, I was unfamiliar with the material properties. I ended up trying to heat up the steel with a propane torch. This proved to not output enough heat to make the metal bendable, and it was not until I started using MAPP gas that I could begin to bend the metal.

I also had a tough time drilling such thick steel in an efficient manner. Order of operations was important in this process. I know that because I did not plan it out well. I bent the sheets before I drilled out the holes. This made it much more difficult because I had to drill on a curved surface.

With this being my introduction into metal work there were a lot of mistakes made, this did, however, lead to a lot of learning. It taught me about gauges and their meaning as well as some of the material properties and how to accommodate for them.
