Shields

I have created many shields out of many different materials. The turtle shell shield is steel, in which I learned how important good weld penetration is especially when the parts are at angles to each other. In some of my earlier welds, I did not get full penetration and the fell apart when I had to bend some of the steel.

The Greek shield was a lesson in fiberglass. There are several layers of fiberglass that make up the shield and they are coated in epoxy. I had not used fiberglass that much until this project, so it was a new process for me. I used an old metal sled for a mold and layered up the different sheets of fiberglass and bonded them together with resin. Once it had hardened, I cut off the excess and began to paint the shield. I wanted the shield to be Greek, so I added a template of a Greek owl out of painter’s tape and then painted it.

The kite shield was made of pine, and it was a good introduction to using wood, which I had not had prior to this. I had very few woodworking tools at this point, so it was difficult to get the look that I was going for. I cut the shape out by using a jigsaw and then sanded down the edges to get a smooth look.
