Swords

This was my first attempt at using hardened steel to make a blade. I started this sword by forging high carbon steel and creating edge bevels. After I had the edges sharp and the handle finished, I went to the last step of hardening and tempering the steel. Unfortunately, I did this part in the middle of the day and could not tell how hot the metal was getting. Because of this, I ended up melting the sword in half. I then proceeded to remake the entire sword again in under five days, but this time, I used the knowledge I had gained from already making a sword to improve the new one. This really taught me how important failure is when it comes to improving a design.

Since this sword I have made two more blades - a Japanese tanto and a camping knife. These were both much easier to make than the sword because I had more experience and because of the smaller size. Because of how much smaller they are, it is easier to get a straight edge on the blade. I am particularly fond of the camping knife because I made it before going on a 3-week camping trip out west where I used it.
